Trojan

Trojan horse or Trojan is a type of malware that can disguise itself as legitimate software. Unlike viruses and worms, trojan's cannot self-replicate.

Instead they need user interaction in order to gain access to computer systems. Once this access has been given, Trojan's can use a variety of methods to spy on you or steal your information.

Things such as:

Backdoor - gives a remote user control of a computer.

Exploit - programs that contain infected data that can take advantage of vulnerabilities in an operating system.

Rootkit - conceal malicious programs from being detected.

Trojan-Banker - steals account data for online banking systems.

Trojan DDOS - sends multiple requests to several infected computers to overwhelm the target address.
